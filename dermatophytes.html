Dermatophytes cause tinea
Trichophyton, Epidermophyton and Microsporum
Dermatophytes infect the skin
Tinea capitis is on the head/scalp
Tinea corporis is on the body
Tinea cruris is in the groin, also known as "jock itch"
Tinea pedis is on the foot, also known as "athlete's foot"
Animals can be a source of transmission
Pruritic lesions
Diagnosed with KOH prep of skin scrapings
Wood's lamp can be used to diagnose Microsporum
Treat tinea with topical -azoles
Dermatophytes can also cause onychomycosis
Terbinafine treats onychomycosis
Griseofulvin treats onychomycosis
